The Incredible Hulk

The Hulk is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in The Incredible Hulk #1 (May 1962). The Hulk is the alter ego of Dr. Bruce Banner, a physically weak and socially withdrawn physicist who transforms into a green-skinned, hulking, and muscular humanoid monster when subjected to emotional stress or physical harm. The character is known for his immense strength, durability, and regenerative healing factor.

The Hulk has been featured in various media adaptations, including television series, animated shows, and live-action films. The character has become one of Marvel's most iconic and enduring superheroes, often depicted as a tragic figure struggling to control his immense power while seeking acceptance and understanding from society.
